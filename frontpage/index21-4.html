Tournament for Life

Most of you know me, but for those who don't my name is Shawn Kobel. My wife, Vicki, and I are having the first ever Relay for Life - Open Bass Tournament and we were hoping you could help us spread the word. Illinois Natural History Survey

Submitted by Mike Blake on Tue, 02/24/2009 - 1:40pm

From April through October 2008, the Illinois Natural History Survey (INHS) and the University of Illinois (U of I) conducted tournament research in coordination with the Ever-Bloom tournament trail at Evergreen and Lakes. The goals of this study were to 1) evaluate delayed mortality (fish that are weighed-in alive but die within 3 days of the tournament) after bass tournaments at Evergreen Lake and 2) evaluate the physiological status (stress) of tournament-caught bass at Lake Bloomington at a seasonal scale.

Ever-Bloom Update

Submitted by Mike Blake on Wed, 01/28/2009 - 2:45pm

Well we are getting closer to the 2009 Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail start, so I thought I would remind and clarify to those interested in fishing how we fill the 25 slots.

First, all teams that fished an event in 2008 have first priority for 2009. If you plan to compete you must submit an entry form and the first tournament fee (in cash) by March 1, 2009 to ensure a spot. The entry form is available on the web page at http://www.everbloom.us or you can pick one up at Terry Brown's office, located at 2204 Eagle Road, Normal. The entry forms can be submitted at Terry's office also.
