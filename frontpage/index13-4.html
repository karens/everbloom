Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 05/26/2008 - 2:13pm
Date: 
Saturday, May 24, 2008 - 6:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington
Source: Flickr

Tougher than whip leather.....Lake Bloomington again proved to be "Lake Doomington" and a tough adversary for the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail competitors May 24.

Twenty five teams corralled 20 fish weighing a measly 46.58 lbs for the event for a average of 2.32 lbs per fish.

Winners were Fred Myers and Ken Petersen with 3 bass weighing 7.46 lbs. They also had a big bass of 4.13 lbs. They extended their lead to almost 6 pounds in the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year Standings with this victory. They caught their bass on a homemade Kenny P Chatterbait and a Reaction Innovations Sweet Beaver.

In second place was Downs anglers Roger Rider and Brian Bounds with 2 bass weighing 5.13 lbs. They currently sit in third place in the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the year standings.

Russell and Ferguson Prevail at Evergreen

The second event of the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail found some tough fishing but the best in Central Illinois always rise to the occasion. East wind and cool temps greeted 21 teams at Evergreen Lake May 10.

Veteran anglers Larry Russell and Randy Ferguson took top honors with 3 bass weighing 8.01 lbs. They were working bed fish pitching jigs and plastic in shallow water. Great job guys!

In second were Roger Rider and Brian Bounds with 2 bass weighing 7.96 lbs. They too caught their bass shallow.

In third and winning 2 Setyr Cranking sticks were Dusty Whitaker and Brian Estes with two bass weighing 7.42 lbs.

Hog Heaven at Lake Bloomington

Submitted by Terry Brown on Sun, 04/27/2008 - 6:29pm
Date: 
Saturday, April 26, 2008 - 6:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington
Source: Flickr

Lake Bloomington stalwarts Dave Norris and Greg Bayles won the first Strike King Ever-Bloom tournament of the year at Lake Bloomington on April 26 with 3 bass weighing 10.62 lbs. They had 3 bass that was anchored by a 6.30 lber. They are leading the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year Championship after one event.

In second place was Ken Petersen and Tom Anderson with two bass weighing 10.03 lbs. They had a big bass of.......get this......7.39 lbs. That is a big fish anywhere and it was caught on a crankbait. As the story goes.....the fish had a single treble hook in the fish and the bait fell out while being netted. Great job guys and now the chase is a tough one for the Sam Leman Big Fish of the Year. The Sam Leman Big Fish of the Year pays $500.00.
