Classic Results

The 2006 Strike King Ever-Bloom Classic was a nail biter. Saturday's starting temperature was 35 degrees and wind gusts were well over 30 mph. To show just how tough the anglers are in Central Illinois there were 20 fish caught for a very respectable 51.85 pounds of bass.

Vicious Fishing Big Bass on day one was caught by Whitacre and Estes and it weighed 5.73 pounds.

The team of Scott Bree and AJ(Flash) Bailey led after the first day with 12.04 lbs followed closely by Richard(you can call me Dusty) Whitacre and Brian Estes with 11.96 pounds. A total of 10 teams caught bass.

Not Much of a Slugfest

The final Strike King Ever-Bloom event at Lake Bloomington on August 19 was anything but a slugfest but Dusty Whitacre and Bryan Estes proved that persistance and grinding all day does pay off. The duo finished the day with the only 5 bass limit that weighed a respectible 11.56 pounds to win. They bettered the team of Larry Andris and Ron Long who weighed 3 fish at 8.86 lbs.

Maisenbacher and Didonato Win at Evergreen

Two guys with names that sound like a Chicago law firm won the July 22nd Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament trail event on Evergreen Lake with 5 fish that weighed 16.77 lbs. They also had another monster bass of 6.19 lbs to take Mossy Oak Big Bass as well. If that big bass holds they will be receiving a years sponsorship from Mossy Oak. Great job again Jamie and Guido! They caught their fish shallow on a Zoom Brushhog.

Whitacre / Estes Victorious

A cool morning with bluebird skies welcomed anglers to the fith stop on the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail on Lake Bloomington.

Many of the tournament fishermen donned windshirts and jackets at the 6:00 am takoff, but the early fishing was anything but cold. Twenty two teams brought 51 fish to the scales that totaled 117.63 pounds. Talk around the scales revealed that a vast percentage of the green guys were caught before the air temperature warmed to the low 80's.
