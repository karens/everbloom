Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 05/16/2011 - 7:49pm
Date: 
Saturday, May 14, 2011 - 6:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington

Bad Weather Again!

Seems like every Rapala Ever-Bloom Tournament has Mother Nature's bad list. Cold temps, high north winds and over 2 inches of rain followed a 80 degree day presenting miserable fishing conditions for the second Rapala Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail event presented by Sam Leman Automotive Group at Lake Bloomington.

No hill for a climber as the father and son team of Randy and Hunter Ferguson had a limit of bass weighing 12.92 to take home the victory. Jamie Maisenbacher and Brad Norris, also with a limit of fish finished second with 11.54 pounds. In third were Duckett Fishing Rod winners Aaron and Mike Therien with 11.01 pounds.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 04/18/2011 - 6:13am
Date: 
Saturday, April 16, 2011 - 6:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Evergreen
Source: Flickr

Brutal conditions met anglers for the first Rapala Ever-Bloom Tournament on Evergreen Lake for 2011. High winds, rain/snow and 38 degrees greeted 23 teams. This was without hesitation this was one of the most brutal days of fishing ever encountered in the 9 year Rapala Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail history.

The Rapala Ever- Bloom Tournament Trail has the best anglers in Central Illinois and big fish was the rule on this super tough day.

Terry Brown and Mike Blake flipped the mid portions of the lake and won the event with 4 fish weighing 12.92 pounds.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 08/16/2010 - 3:46am
Date: 
Saturday, August 14, 2010 - 5:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington
Source: Flickr

The Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament trail got a "big show" on Saturday August 14 as Brian Bounds and John Warmbir smoked the field with 22.97 pounds of bass to take the next to last regular season event. They won by over 14 pounds! They had the only limit and also had big bass of the tournament with a 6.45 pounder. That big bass was also the Sam Leman Big Bass of the Year so far in 2010. This is the largest 5 bass limit in SKEBTT history besting Tim Fauth and Scott Anderson's Evergreen limit of 21.97 pounds in 2006. Congrats guys and what a day!

In second place was Brad Norris and Jamie Masenbacher with 7.99 pounds followed by Brian Estes and Dusty Whitacre with 7.79 pounds.
