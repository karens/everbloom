Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 07/27/2009 - 11:50am
Date: 
Saturday, July 25, 2009 - 5:00am to 1:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington
Source: Flickr

The Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail visited Lake Bloomington on July 25 greeted by a cold front and wind, however the lake produced great fishing and 54 keepers were brought to the scales weighing 130.50 pounds. WOW! Starting at 5:15 am jackets were a must but temps later in the day rose to near 80 and fishing was even hotter. This lake is no longer Lake Doomington.

The team of Jamie Maisenbacher and Brad Norris bested the 20 boat field with 4 bass weighing 14.07 anchored by a 5.36 pound largemouth. They fished docks and grass. We would imagine a Zoom Brush Hog played here as well. Great job guys.

In second place was the ever steady Dusty Whitacre and Jeff Myers with 5 bass weighing 12.88 pounds.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Sun, 06/28/2009 - 5:20am
Date: 
Saturday, June 27, 2009 - 5:00am to 1:00pm
Location: 
Lake Evergreen
Source: Flickr

Saturday June 27 was an exceptional fishing day for Terry Brown and Mike Blake at the Strike King EverBloom Tournament on Evergreen Lake. They had a 5 bass limit that weighed 19.06 pounds anchored by a giant 6.13 pounder fishing points and ledges on the main basin of Evergreen Lake with jigs and plastics. The team caught 14 keeper fish during the day and had their limit by 7:30 am.

In second place was super steady Jamie Maisenbacher and Brad Norris with 5 bass weighing 13.53 pounds. They caught their limit pitch and flipping wood and grass with Nichols Jigs and Strike King Rage Tail trailers.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Mike Blake on Fri, 05/29/2009 - 7:00pm
Date: 
Saturday, May 30, 2009 - 5:00am to 1:00pm
Location: 
Lake Evergreen
Source: Flickr

The Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail completed the last event Saturday and the conditions were perfect. Warm temps, light winds, an earlier start time, 5:00 am and 15 boats looking for both spawning and post spawn bass.

2008 Team of the Year Kenny Petersen and Fred Myers had a great day with 5 bass weighing 13.38 pounds to take the victory. Their day started out tough as they reported losing 5 keepers before 8:30 am. They fished mid lake areas and caught their fish on a Mr. P, a chatterbiat designed and made by Petersen, a Zoom Horny Toad and a Reaction Innovations Sweet Beaver. Great job again guys. They are leading the AOY race again boys. Time to get after them.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Submitted by Terry Brown on Mon, 05/18/2009 - 7:05am
Date: 
Saturday, May 16, 2009 - 6:00am to 2:00pm
Location: 
Lake Bloomington
Source: Flickr

Another tough weather day for the Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail. Week 1 was, as many remember, cold, windy and rainy. That tournament was won by Jerry Martoglio and Steve Anderson with the lowest winning weight in Ever-Bloom history with two fish weighing 5.36 lbs. They also had big bass of 3.19 pounds.

This weekend's event was a mirror image of the first event. Cold temps, misty rain that gave away to bluebird skies and heavy northwest winds. Winds were estimated at 30 mph but 8 teams still managed to catch bass on Lake Doomington. Several anglers noted that they caught numerous 10-12 inch bass and several also had walleyes. Problem with that is we were searching for 15" bass and walleye do not count. They do taste great after sitting in hot grease however!
