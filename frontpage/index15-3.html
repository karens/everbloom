Sixth Event - Sixth Winners

On a beautiful sun-kissed Saturday in August, the sixth event of the 2008 Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail on Lake Evergreen was captured by Domenic Theison and Tom Cassel.

Even though the weather was ideal, the bass fishing was tough. Cassel and Theison ground out a limit of bass that weighed in at 11:02 lbs and according to Tom, they even culled one fish.

Second place went to the team of Loren Peterson and Mike Blake with 4 fish that totaled 10:31 lbs. Peters and Blake had 4 keepers by 9:00 am but could not corral another in the remaining 5 hours.

Brown and Blake win at Bloomington

Mike Blake and Terry Brown won the July 26th Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament at Lake Bloomington with a 5 bass limit weighing 13.66 lbs. They caught their bass on Omega and Jewel Jigs with a Zoom Super Speed Craw and Strike King Rage craw trailer, respectively, A Zoom Worm, and a Gene Larew Mega-Ring tube in both shallow and deep water. They had the only limit of the event.

In second were the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year leaders, Fred Myers and Ken Petersen who caught their 4 bass weighing 10.27 lbs on a Mr.P Chatterbait and a frog. Myers and Petersen also are the Sam Leman Big Bass of the Year leaders with a 7.39 lber. Consistency in all tournaments has them in the leadership position.

Evergreen Tough on Bloomers

Just about every Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament we get some kind of goofy weather and this event was no different. Heavy rain showers were followed up with bluebird skies and high wind. Being determined and grinding them out was the rule of the day.

There were 26 bass brought to the scales that weighed 69.47 lbs for the day.

When you only catch two bass you had better catch big ones and Andy Wegner and Aaron Geheber did just that at the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament at Evergreen Lake over the weekend. They landed two bass that weighed 9.22 lbs and had big fish of 5.96 lbs. They caught their bass fishing shallow.

In second were Jamie Maisenbacher and Brad Norris with 3 fish at 7.33 lbs. They also caught their fish shallow.

Tougher than whip leather.....Lake Bloomington again proved to be "Lake Doomington" and a tough adversary for the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail competitors May 24.

Twenty five teams corralled 20 fish weighing a measly 46.58 lbs for the event for a average of 2.32 lbs per fish.

Winners were Fred Myers and Ken Petersen with 3 bass weighing 7.46 lbs. They also had a big bass of 4.13 lbs. They extended their lead to almost 6 pounds in the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year Standings with this victory. They caught their bass on a homemade Kenny P Chatterbait and a Reaction Innovations Sweet Beaver.

In second place was Downs anglers Roger Rider and Brian Bounds with 2 bass weighing 5.13 lbs. They currently sit in third place in the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the year standings.

Russell and Ferguson Prevail at Evergreen

The second event of the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail found some tough fishing but the best in Central Illinois always rise to the occasion. East wind and cool temps greeted 21 teams at Evergreen Lake May 10.

Veteran anglers Larry Russell and Randy Ferguson took top honors with 3 bass weighing 8.01 lbs. They were working bed fish pitching jigs and plastic in shallow water. Great job guys!

In second were Roger Rider and Brian Bounds with 2 bass weighing 7.96 lbs. They too caught their bass shallow.

In third and winning 2 Setyr Cranking sticks were Dusty Whitaker and Brian Estes with two bass weighing 7.42 lbs.
