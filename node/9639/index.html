Tough Day for a Norris

The 7th event saw a 7th different winner and there is no question that parity is alive and well on the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail. It was a bright, warm, bluebird day and one Norris needed a few ounces to overtake another one on Lake Bloomington and the 7th Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail. Which Norris took home the gold? Which Norris needed the weight of one paperclip. Did the old man beat the youngster or did the youngster prevail? The Results are in.... Brad Norris and his partner Jamie Maisenbacher took home the win by a mere 4 hundredth's of the a pound over Lake Bloomington stalwarts Dave Norris and Greg Bayles. B Norris and Maisenbacher weighed 4 bass for an 8.95 total and D Norris and Bayles weighed 4 for 8.91 lbs. Third place finishers were the team of Matt Rice and Rick McMahan with 2 bass weighing 7.92 lbs followed closely by AJ, My socks are too long, Bailey and Scott Bree with 4 that weighed 7.48 lbs. Rounding out the top 5 were Terry Brown and Mike Blake with 3 bass that weighed 6.30 lbs. McMahan and Rice took home two Setyr Drop Shot rods for their 3rd place showing. Docks and sea walls were the key targets and tubes, shaky heads and jigs were the baits of choice. The Classic and Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year Standings...... The SKEBTT Classic will be October 4 and 5 and all participants must have their entries paid before the event to be involved. The Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year standings have really tightened up. Fred Myers and Ken Petersen still lead with 45.77 lbs. Brown and Blake are second with 41.87 lbs and in third are Norris and Bayles with 39.25 lbs. Dusty Whitacre and Brian Estes are 4th with 36.33 lbs and Andy Wegner and Triple X Aaron Geheber are 5th with 32.85 lbs. this is going down to the wire! Entry is $120.00 and can be paid at the Wired2Fish Office. Telephone number is 452-0313 if you have any questions
