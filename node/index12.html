By Mike Blake, 24 February, 2009

Illinois Natural History Survey

From April through October 2008, the Illinois Natural History Survey (INHS) and the University of Illinois (U of I) conducted tournament research in coordination with the Ever-Bloom tournament trail at Evergreen and Lakes. The goals of this study were to 1) evaluate delayed mortality (fish that are weighed-in alive but die within 3 days of the tournament) after bass tournaments at Evergreen Lake and 2) evaluate the physiological status (stress) of tournament-caught bass at Lake Bloomington at a seasonal scale.

By Mike Blake, 28 January, 2009

Ever-Bloom Update

Well we are getting closer to the 2009 Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail start, so I thought I would remind and clarify to those interested in fishing how we fill the 25 slots. First, all teams that fished an event in 2008 have first priority for 2009. If you plan to compete you must submit an entry form and the first tournament fee (in cash) by March 1, 2009 to ensure a spot. The entry form is available on the web page at https://everbloom.us or you can pick one up at Terry Brown's office, located at 2204 Eagle Road, Normal.

Martoglio and Anderson win SKEBTT Classic

Veteran anglers Jerry Martoglio and Steve Anderson took top honors in the October 4 and 5 Strike King Ever-Bloom Classic on Evergreen and Lake Bloomington respectively. They were the 8th different team to win an event in 2008. Parity is indeed alive and well at the SKEBTT. They weighed 6 bass of 13.29 lbs for the honor. They edged out Jamie Maisenbacher and Brad Norris with 11.42 lbs and Terry Brown and Mike Blake with 10.84 lbs.

Sixth Event - Sixth Winners

On a beautiful sun-kissed Saturday in August, the sixth event of the 2008 Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail on Lake Evergreen was captured by Domenic Theison and Tom Cassel. Even though the weather was ideal, the bass fishing was tough. Cassel and Theison ground out a limit of bass that weighed in at 11:02 lbs and according to Tom, they even culled one fish. Second place went to the team of Loren Peterson and Mike Blake with 4 fish that totaled 10:31 lbs.

Brown and Blake win at Bloomington

Mike Blake and Terry Brown won the July 26th Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament at Lake Bloomington with a 5 bass limit weighing 13.66 lbs. They caught their bass on Omega and Jewel Jigs with a Zoom Super Speed Craw and Strike King Rage craw trailer, respectively, A Zoom Worm, and a Gene Larew Mega-Ring tube in both shallow and deep water. They had the only limit of the event. In second were the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year leaders, Fred Myers and Ken Petersen who caught their 4 bass weighing 10.27 lbs on a Mr.P Chatterbait and a frog.

Evergreen Tough on Bloomers

Just about every Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament we get some kind of goofy weather and this event was no different. Heavy rain showers were followed up with bluebird skies and high wind. Being determined and grinding them out was the rule of the day. There were 26 bass brought to the scales that weighed 69.47 lbs for the day. When you only catch two bass you had better catch big ones and Andy Wegner and Aaron Geheber did just that at the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament at Evergreen Lake over the weekend.

Tougher than whip leather.....Lake Bloomington again proved to be "Lake Doomington" and a tough adversary for the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail competitors May 24. Twenty five teams corralled 20 fish weighing a measly 46.58 lbs for the event for a average of 2.32 lbs per fish. Winners were Fred Myers and Ken Petersen with 3 bass weighing 7.46 lbs. They also had a big bass of 4.13 lbs. They extended their lead to almost 6 pounds in the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year Standings with this victory.
