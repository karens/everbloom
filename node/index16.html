By Terry Brown, 28 September, 2005

Thanks to Everyone

Thanks to everyone that fished the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament trail again this year. Without you it would not be a success and hopefully we will have water back in Bloomington for next years classic. Photos' of the classic are now up on the site. We hope you enjoy them as well. I send information to our sponsors after each event and do a year-end recap to keep them appraised of what we do and how we do it. It is very important when you go out and purchase tackle and products to include our sponsors if possible.

Ever-Bloom Classic

Date
Sat, Sep 24 2005 7:00 AM - Sun, Sep 25 2005 3:00 AM

September 24-25 Ever-Bloom Classic Results

Bailey and Bree Win Classic in Tough Conditions

Tough fishing conditions met 18 Classic Teams from the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament trail and consistency was tough to come by. One team was very consistent and as a result won the event. Catching 6 bass that weighed an impressive 21.61 lbs AJ Bailey and Scott Bree found the technique and the right fish to win bettering the field by over 5 lbs. They also had big fish on day two at 4.11 lbs.

Blanking the 1st day and then putting on a fish catching show were veteran anglers Jerry Martoglio and Loren Peters. Their second day weight of 5 fish weighing 15.43 lbs was good enough for second place.

Rounding out the final 5 were Manny Marquez and Andy Spanier, David Fritts and Jeff Morrison; first day leaders, and Larry Andris and Ron Long. Andris and Long had big fish on day 1 with a giant 6.67 lber.

Tournament packages were given to each classic qualifier. Thanks to BassFan.com, Terminator, Tru Tungsten, Strike King, Ranger Boats, Skeeter Boats, Yamaha Marine, Gemini Sport Marketing, BassMedics Rejuvenade, Setyr Rods, Plano Tackle Systems and Ultimate Lure Saver for providing product for these packages. A special thanks to Midstate Fish and Feather for a great banquet meal at the classic awards banquet.

There were 39 bass caught weighing 121.47 lbs.

The schedule will be out soon. Stay tuned to the Ever-Bloom website for details.

Thanks to all who fished with us this year.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Aug 20 2005 7:00 AM

August 20, 2005 Results

Results

Martoglio and Peters Dominate Evergreen....

Veteran anglers Jerry Martoglio and Loren Peters smoked the field with the only 5 bass limit that weighed and unbelievable 18.14 lbs and a big bass of 4.52 lbs. They didn't travel far from the launch site and utilized crankbaits around wood for the victory. This is the second win in a row for Martoglio who teamed up with Tommy Kaufman in the last win at Evergreen also with a limit.

Larry Andris and Ron Long finished 2nd with 2 bass weighing 8.23 lbs including big bass of the tournament weighing 4.92 lbs

Bryan Medlin and Mason Wright captured 3rd with 7.50 lbs and received the Strike King "top to bottom" lure package for their efforts.

The Strike King EverBloom Classic will be September 24th and 25th. The 24th will be at Bloomington and 25th at Evergreen. Midwest Fish and Feather will be providing a meal on the final day. Thanks for participating all year and we look forward to even a bigger and better Classic.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jul 23 2005 7:00 AM

July 23, 2005 Results

Veterans Martoglio and Kaufman win Evergreen.........

Hot, sultry, July weather with temps in the mid 90's met 16 teams at the 6th qualifier of the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail at Evergreen Lake. Forcasted temps of over 100 degrees did not materialize as a hazy cloud cover and breeze of 5-10 miles an hour made it comfortable for the anglers but the fish seemed to have a bit of "lockjaw". Only one limit was caught, surprising everyone, as fishing has been redhot as late at the 960 acre impoundment.

Veteran anglers Jerry Martoglio and Tommy Kaufman won the event with 5 bass weighing 14.01 lbs and weighed the only limit of the tournament. Fishing shallow wood with tubes and jigs was the ticket for the "old timers". Way to go guys!!!

In second was Evergreen regulars Jim Sherman and Ron Bristow with 3 bass of 10.22 and big bass of 4.40 lbs. They were fishing points utilizing jigs and crankbaits.

In third and winning the Strike King "top to bottom tackle packages" were Dave Fritts and Robin Sprinkle with 8.55 lbs. Tackle packs included products from Ultimate Lure Saver, Mossy Oak Fishing Line, Terminator, and Strike King.

Rounding out the top 5 were Scott Anderson and Tim Fauth with 2 bass at 6.38 lbs and Terry Brown and Mike Blake with 5.75 lbs.

There were 21 bass weighing 58.90 lbs of bass weighed and all were released alive.

The next event will be August 20 again at Evergreen and will serve as a tune-up for the Strike King Ever-Bloom Classic September 25/26. Remember you have to fish at least 4 events to make the Classic and you will not want to miss it!! Midwest Fish and Feather will again be supplying a meal at the Classic.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jun 25 2005 7:00 AM

June 25, 2005 Results

Nearly 160 lbs of Bass........Lake Bloomington shows its true colors

Sixteen teams fought brutal heat of over 90 degrees and minimal wind but the real heat was on the water. Bass bit and bit well with 61 bass weighing nearly 160 lbs that breaks every record for any event in recent memory. Awesome is the only way to describe the weigh-in with only one team blanking but they actually had fish and chose not to weigh them as a result of the heavy sacks previously weighing in. The difference in the first several places were kicker fish. Those that caught one did well. There were 8 limits weighed in. There were no dead bass. Thanks to all for caring for your catch and thanks to BassMedics for making a great product Rejuvenade. There is no doubt it made a big difference.

Dusty Whitacre and Tom Lindsey took 1st place and big bass fishing a myriad of baits and actually homesteading one area most of the day. Their total weight was an amazing 16.14 lbs and their big fish weighed an equally impressive 5.78 lbs. Looks like this team is back on a roll.

In second was one of the hottest teams in the area Tim Fauth and Scott Anderson caught them again and weighed 5 bass of 14.61 lbs.

The third place team of Aaron Geheber and Andy Wegner won the Strike King Top to Bottom Award with a great bag of 14.02 lbs. They also had a big fish of 4.91 lbs.

Rounding out the top 5 were Terry Brown and Mike Blake with 5 bass weighing 12.38 lbs and Jerry Martoglio and Loren Peters with 11.91 lbs.

Special thanks to Loren and Jerry for helping with the weigh in.

There were 61 bass caught weighing 157.37 lbs.

Our next event is July 23rd at Lake Evergreen.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jun 11 2005 7:00 AM

June 11, 2005 Results

* Two teams were docked 1 pound for dead fish, boats 9 and 4. The results in the chart reflect the actual, not reduced weight of the fish.

Fauth and Anderson Dominate Evergreen.....

No cold fronts, no rain, and perfect conditions met the 4th installment of the Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament Trail competitors June 11.

Tim Fauth and Scott Anderson dominated the field with one of the largest 5 bass limits in recent memory at Evergreen of 20.11 lbs anchored with a big bass of 4.94 lbs. That is a "Toad Sack" in anybodies book!!! Way to go guys!! They caught their fish on a jig and chunk and culled a 4 lber to boot. This is the second win in a row for this consistent duo. They won our last event on Lake Bloomington.

In second were Hudsonites Dave Fritts and Jeff Morrison with an equally impressive 5 fish limit weighing 13.35 lbs. They caught their fish in the mid lake on a variety of baits.

The Strike King "top to bottom" and first out of the money winners in third place were Mike Blake and Terry Brown with 3 fish weighing 7.60 lbs. Looks like the bags are ready for the next event.

Fourth place went to veteran anglers Tom Kaufman and Jerry Martoglio.

There were a total of 24 bass caught that weighed 67.90 lbs.

We have a new sponsor, Mossy Oak Fishing Line has joined our impressive line-up and will be providing line for top to bottom bags and Classic prizes as well. Remember to buy our sponsors products. They allow us to put on this trail and your support is necessary for them to continue.

Our next event is June 25 at Lake Bloomington. Please contact us if you plan on fishing. You can reach Terry at 309-452-0313 if you have any questions. Talk to some friends and have them join us!

 

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, May 28 2005 1:00 PM

May 28, 2005 Results

Fauth and Anderson Win Bloomington Strike King Ever-Bloom

The 3rd Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament blew in on the 28th of May on Lake Bloomington....no cold front this time but plenty of wind. It looks like fishing is on the rise and the team of Tim Fauth and Scott Anderson won the event with 4 fish weighing 10.55 lbs. They had a big fish of 3.15 lbs in their bag. Fauth and Anderson caught their fish of soft plastic twitch baits fishing windy points. There was plenty of them with winds gusting to 25 mphs in the afternoon. Tim Fauth finally trashed his Corona hat and they got a few fish in the boat as a result. Feel free to ask him if he will ever wear it again.

Second place team Scott Bree and AJ, Mr. Secretary, Bailey had 4 fish weighing *8.55 lbs. *They had a single one pound dead fish penalty. They threw a myriad of different baits and this team always seem to find fish....and put them in the boat.

Dave Norris fishing solo finished 3rd with 4 fish weighing 7.16 lbs. Maybe Greg Bayles will get a hall pass from the war department for Evergreen and help his partner at the next event. Looks like we did find out which member catches the fish on this saavy team.

Big Fish was caught by Larry Russell and Mike Campbell and it weighed a respectable 3.39 lbs.

A total of 19 teams weighed 23 fish for a total of 54.96 lbs.

The next event is June 11 at Lake Evergreen. Please contact Mike or Terry if you plan on fishing.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, May 14 2005 7:00 AM

May 14, 2005 Results

Imagine a Strike-King Ever-Bloom tournament and no cold front. Imagine again......

Another Midwestern cold front with 15-25 mph WNW winds and tough fishing conditions met 22 teams in the 2nd qualifier of the SKEBTT. Water temps in the low 60's and pre-spawn and spawn fish was the buzz before the event but that all changed.

Randy Ferguson and Larry Russell figured them out flipping plastic to grass and wood and brought 3 nice fish to the scales weighing 11.46 lbs and a first place check. They also had the big fish of the event weighing 4.39 lbs.

Terry Brown and Mike Blake took second place with three fish weighing 7.32 lbs. They caught their bass on wood and grass on plastics as well.

Scott Bree fishing solo took the Strike King "Top to Bottom" one out of the money tackle packs with two bass weighing 6.85 lbs.

The big catch of the day went to James Hurt who caught a 17.87 lb flathead catfish on a jig.

There were 14 bass brought to scales weighing 44.18 for a 3.15 lb average.

 

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Apr 30 2005 7:00 AM

April 30, 2005 Results

Norris and Bayles on a Roll

When you get on a roll, you can't do anything wrong and so far this year in numerous events Dave Norris and Greg Bayles have been unstoppable. Blustery, cold front conditions seen last year in the first three events again appeared for the 1st Strike King Ever-Bloom Qualifier on Lake Bloomington April 30 with temperatures at take-off in the upper 40's.

Cold fronts in Central Illinois notoriously makes the bite difficult but not for the two saavy Normal residents. As always this is a 100% cash payout trail and Norris and Bayles parlayed that into a check for $520.00. Caught while cranking and weighing an impressive 5 fish limit of 13.22 allowed them to outdistance the runner-up team of Loren Peters and Jerry Martoglio who had 4 fish weighing 11.67. Their bag was anchored by a 5.58 lb largemouth. Second place paid $280 and big bass paid $200.00. First out of the money with 4 fish weighing 9.42 lbs were newcomers Nick Cira and James Hurt who won the "Strike King Top to Bottom" bag featuring Strike King, Kinami, Terminator, and Castrol products. They were also awarded a Strike King Ever-Bloom bump board provided by Extra Edge Products. Ask them how they got it at the next tournament!!

Twenty boats caught 34 bass weighing 87.02 lbs for a 2.56 lb average.
