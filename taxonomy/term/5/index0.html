Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Aug 28 2010 6:00 AM - Sat, Aug 28 2010 2:00 PM
Location
Lake Evergreen

Flickr photos from the August 28, 2010 album.

The last regular season event of the Strike King Ever-Bloom TT was held August 28. Super low water conditions with the lake dropping nearly 5 feet in the last two weeks took much of the vegetation out of the equation, but as always the teams in this trail rose to the occasion with two limits and two 12 pound sacks of bass were brought to the scales. Red hot Dave Whalen and Ryan Robinson won the event with 5 bass weighing 12.97 pounds. They were followed closely by another super hot team of Brian Bounds and John Hodge with 5 bass weighing 12.64 pounds.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Aug 14 2010 5:00 AM - Sat, Aug 14 2010 2:00 PM
Location
Lake Bloomington

Flickr photos from the August 14, 2010 album.

The Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament trail got a "big show" on Saturday August 14 as Brian Bounds and John Warmbir smoked the field with 22.97 pounds of bass to take the next to last regular season event. They won by over 14 pounds! They had the only limit and also had big bass of the tournament with a 6.45 pounder. That big bass was also the Sam Leman Big Bass of the Year so far in 2010. This is the largest 5 bass limit in SKEBTT history besting Tim Fauth and Scott Anderson's Evergreen limit of 21.97 pounds in 2006.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jul 17 2010 5:30 AM - Sat, Jul 17 2010 1:30 PM
Location
Lake Evergreen

The Strike King EverBloom Tournament Trail event on Lake Evergreen was a tale of two days. The fishing day was very hot and humid with temperatures in the 90's. At 2 pm a major storm blew in with heavy rain and lightning but the anglers from the SKEBTT proved tougher than the weather.

Rain soaked weigh ins are not fun but just about every team braved the elements to stay and watch. Thanks to Pat Welch, Dusty Whitacre and Mike Blake for making sure we got the fish weighed in and back into the water. Tough conditions for sure.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jun 26 2010 5:30 AM - Sat, Jun 26 2010 1:30 PM
Location
Lake Bloomington

Flickr photos from the June 26, 2010 album.

The 5th Strike King Ever-Bloom Tournament was held June 26 at Lake Bloomington. It started out with high humidity, stormy conditions and overcast skies and the bass were ready. Several bass over 5 pounds were weighed in and it was a slugfest. Terry Brown and Mike Blake made it two in a row with a 5 bass limit weighing 19.62 pounds anchored by a 5.44 big bass. In second was the team of Brian Bounds and John Hodge with 5 bass weighing 18.86 pounds. Third place was Brad Norris and Jamie Maisenbacher with 4 bass weighing 12.90 pounds.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Jun 12 2010 6:00 AM - Sat, Jun 12 2010 2:00 PM
Location
Lake Evergreen

Flickr photos from the June 12, 2010 album.

The Strike King EverBloom Tournament Trail competed on June 12 at Evergreen Lake. Hot humid conditions with little wind greeted the anglers. Terry Brown and Mike Blake won with 5 bass weighing 17.32 pounds. They also had big bass of the event with a 5.44 pounder. They currently lead the Sam Leman Heavyweight of the Year standings with 34.71. Dave Whalen and Ryan Robinson are in second with 32.56 pounds. In second was the team of Dusty Whitacre and Brian Estes with a 5 bass limit of 15.33 pounds. They were followed by Randy Ferguson and Larry Russell with a 5 bass limit weighing 13.67.

Ever-Bloom Tournament

Date
Sat, Apr 17 2010 6:00 AM - Sat, Apr 17 2010 2:00 PM
Location
Lake Bloomington

Flickr photos from the April 17, 2010 album.

The first event of the Strike King EverBloom Tournament Trail is in the books. Cold front conditions following an awesome week, weather wise, caused a tough bite and no limits were brought to the scales. Expectations were high but the results were weak in the opener. Perrenial hammers on Lake Bloomington, Fred Myers and Ken Petersen, brought two fish to the scales weighing 7.51 pounds to seal the victory. As usual, they told us they caught them on jigs and chatterbaits. Might be a good idea to come up with another story guys for the next event.
